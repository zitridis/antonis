Antonios Zitridis

Welcome to my webpage!

My name is Antonios Zitridis, and I am a 6th-year PhD student in mathematics at the University of Chicago. My advisor is Panagiotis E. Souganidis. I am originally from Greece and received a B.S. degree in mathematics from the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens.

Starting in Fall 2025, I will be a Byrne Assistant Professor in the Department of Mathematics at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, working within Professor Erhan Bayraktar's research group.

I am interested in Partial Differential Equations, Stochastic Analysis, and their applications. My PhD research is on interacting particle systems, McKean-Vlasov SDEs, stochastic optimal control, and mean field games.

Research

  • Stochastic optimal control and its connection to PDEs.
  • Mean field games and interacting particle systems.
  • Applications to physics and finance, including McKean-Vlasov SDEs and homogenization theory.

Talks & Travel

I actively participate in conferences and workshops, where I give talks on my research topics. I also engage in collaborative projects with researchers around the world, sharing knowledge and exchanging ideas.

Teaching & Mentoring

I am passionate about teaching and mentoring students. My approach focuses on creating an interactive and engaging environment that encourages students to think critically and approach problems with confidence. Over the years, I have taught and mentored students at various levels, from undergraduate to graduate courses.

Courses Taught

  • Calculus I, II, III (Undergraduate)
  • Advanced Linear Algebra (Graduate)
  • Mathematical Methods for Physicists (Graduate)
  • Introduction to Stochastic Processes (Graduate)

Mentoring Experience

As a mentor, I have guided students through research projects, helping them develop essential mathematical and analytical skills. I focus on fostering a collaborative atmosphere where students feel comfortable discussing their ideas and challenges. I have also mentored students in the Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU) program at the University of Chicago.

Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is centered on the idea that mathematics is not just about solving problems but also about understanding the underlying concepts. I encourage students to ask questions, explore different methods, and appreciate the beauty of mathematical thinking.
